## 1. Market Overview

Muscat is Oman's capital — a dramatically beautiful city between mountains and sea. Unlike Dubai or Doha, Muscat is laid-back, culturally rich, and focuses on heritage tourism rather than mega-entertainment. More traditional Gulf atmosphere.

### Key Statistics
- **Population:** ~1.5 million (country ~5M)
- **Character:** Traditional, scenic, relaxed
- **Currency:** Omani Rial (OMR) — highest value GCC currency
- **Key Identity:** "Pearl of Arabia", mountains & sea

### Key Differences from Dubai
| Factor | Dubai | Muscat |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Pace | Fast, 24/7 | Slow, relaxed |
| Entertainment | Mega clubs, nightlife | Hotel bars, cultural |
| Architecture | Modern skyscrapers | Low-rise, traditional |
| Tourism | Mass tourism | Boutique, nature |
| Alcohol | Widely available | Hotels only |

## 2. Areas & Neighborhoods

### Old Muscat (Mutrah)
- **Vibe:** Historic, authentic, traditional
- **Landmarks:** Mutrah Souq, Corniche, Al Alam Palace
- **Dining:** Traditional Omani, fish market
- **Entertainment:** Evening strolls, souq shopping
- **Best For:** Heritage, authentic experience
- **Price Level:** $–$$

### Qurum
- **Vibe:** Upscale residential, beach, nature
- **Landmarks:** Qurum Beach, Natural Park
- **Accommodation:** Crowne Plaza, mid-range hotels
- **Dining:** Mix of restaurants
- **Entertainment:** Beach, cafes
- **Best For:** Beach access, central location
- **Price Level:** $$–$$$

### Shatti Al Qurum
- **Vibe:** Luxury hotels, diplomatic
- **Landmarks:** Grand Hyatt, InterContinental
- **Accommodation:** 5-star hotels
- **Dining:** Hotel restaurants
- **Entertainment:** Hotel bars, beach
- **Best For:** Luxury stay, nightlife
- **Price Level:** $$$$

### Al Mouj (The Wave)
- **Vibe:** Modern marina, expat hub
- **Landmarks:** Marina, Kempinski, golf
- **Accommodation:** Kempinski, St. Regis (coming)
- **Dining:** Waterfront restaurants
- **Entertainment:** Marina walks, cafes
- **Best For:** Modern, marina living
- **Price Level:** $$$$

### Muttrah Corniche
- **Vibe:** Waterfront promenade, scenic
- **Landmarks:** Fish market, dhow harbor
- **Dining:** Waterfront restaurants
- **Entertainment:** Evening walks
- **Best For:** Photography, heritage

## 8. Local Knowledge

### Transport
- **Taxis:** Metered, Mwasalat app
- **Driving:** Best way to explore (rent car)
- **No Metro:** Car-dependent city
- **Uber:** Not available

### Alcohol
- Hotels only
- More conservative than Dubai
- Licensed restaurants limited
- 21+ age

### Dress Code
- Most conservative GCC country
- Cover shoulders/knees always
- Beach: Hotel beaches more relaxed
- Mosque: Full coverage required

### Cultural Notes
- Very traditional, respectful culture
- Sultan highly respected
- Slower pace of life
- Nature/heritage tourism focus
- Less nightlife than other GCC cities
- Thursday = start of weekend

### Best Time to Visit
- **Oct–Apr:** Pleasant weather
- **Jun–Sep:** Very hot
- **Khareef (monsoon):** Salalah only (Jul–Sep)
